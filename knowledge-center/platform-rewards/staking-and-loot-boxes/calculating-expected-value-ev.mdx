---

### What is Expected Value (EV)? (Simple Explanation)

Expected Value (EV) is a way to figure out what you can expect to get **on average** when you open a Loot Box, based on the chances of getting different rewards.

Think of it like this:

- A Loot Box has different prizes inside.
- Some prizes are common and not worth a lot.
- Some prizes are rare and worth a lot.
- Each prize has a **drop rate** (how often you get it).

Since you don’t know exactly what you’ll get when you open one, **EV helps you figure out the average value of a Loot Box if you opened thousands of them**.

### How to Calculate EV (Step-by-Step)

1. **Look at the possible rewards and their drop rates.**
    Example:
   - A **common item** (60% chance) is worth **$2**
   - A **rare item** (25% chance) is worth **$5**
   - An **epic item** (10% chance) is worth **$15**
   - A **legendary item** (5% chance) is worth **$50**
2. **Multiply each item’s value by its drop rate.**
   - Common: **60% × $2 = $1.20**
   - Rare: **25% × $5 = $1.25**
   - Epic: **10% × $15 = $1.50**
   - Legendary: **5% × $50 = $2.50**
3. **Add everything up to get the total EV**
   - **EV**=1.20+1.25+1.50+2.50=**$6.45** 

### What Does This Mean?

- If you open **just one** Loot Box, you could get **lucky** (a legendary) or **unlucky** (a common).
- But if you open **thousands** of them, the **average** value per box will be **around $6.45**.
- This does **not** mean every box is worth $6.45 - just that, over time, statistically it evens out.
